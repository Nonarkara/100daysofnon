An architect reads a site before drawing a line. Not with an opinion — with relationships. What touches what. What moves through. Where the weight finally lands. The opinion comes later, if at all, and it is cheap.

Most decisions are that same kind of problem, badly lit. These are the lenses I reach for when the light is bad — borrowed from architecture, anthropology, Daoist strategy, and twenty years of building things that had to stand up. Not theories. Tools. Each one is a way of seeing structure where other people see noise.
