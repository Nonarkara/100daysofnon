Method

How the project works. What it promises. What it refuses. The rules of the museum, written down, so that nothing surprises you later. Read this before you read the rest.

Memory and public record agree roughly 60% of the time.
01 / The premise

Memory and record, side by side.

For one hundred days, one question per day. I answer in writing, dated. The answer is then fact-checked against the public record — archives, photographs, third-party sources, and where applicable, satellite imagery.

Every claim is tagged with its status. Memory says one thing. The record says another or says nothing. Where they disagree is the most interesting cell on the page. That is the project.

The disagreement is not lying. It is the mind's grammar reaching for nearby anchors. The grammar is the subject.

02 / Claim types

Four categories. The fourth is the most honest.

Every claim extracted from the day's entry is given one of four ratings:

✓
Verified
Public record agrees. Source cited inline.
✗
Contradicted
Public record disagrees. The disagreement is shown, not hidden.
?
Unverified
Public record is silent so far. May be resolvable. Marked open.
∅
Un-verifiable
Cannot be checked, ever. Internal states, undated childhood, the felt quality of an afternoon. The honest entries live here.

03 / The bot

A second-brain docent. Frontend ready. Backend coming.

A chat interface lives in this museum. It speaks in Dr Non's voice, drawing from the 100-post nonharvard blog corpus (~240,000 words) and from the daily entries as they accumulate.

The bot follows four answer kinds:

  • DIRECT When the answer is in the record, it is given with a citation. Day N, blog · post title.
  • DEDUCED When the answer can be deduced across multiple posts, it is given with a hedge and the closest citation.
  • STRUCTURAL When the answer is not in the record, the bot does not invent it. Instead, it offers a structural guess: not "who killed X" but "who in which position would most want X killed". The shape of the answer is also an answer.
  • NOT KNOWN When even a structural guess is out of scope, the bot says so plainly and offers a question the record could answer instead.

Current status: frontend complete with ~25 pre-loaded demo answers. The Claude API wiring is the next deploy. Until then, the bot answers your free-text input with a best-match from the demo set, and labels itself as unwired.

04 / What this museum is not

Not a portfolio. Not a CV. Not a brand exercise.

A portfolio shows the best of you. This museum shows the dated, fact-checked version of you. Where the two disagree, the portfolio is selling and the museum is reading.

A CV lists what an institution validated. This museum lists what the record can corroborate. Where the two disagree, the CV is the resume and the museum is the document.

A brand exercise concludes. This museum opens. Every room ends on an unfinished question. The bot, when wired, will not conclude either. The journal is a journey, which is to say: it has a starting line and no finish.
