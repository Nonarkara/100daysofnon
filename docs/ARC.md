# 100 Days of Non — Question Arc

Nine phases. One question per day. Each phase interrogates a different layer of the subject. Questions sharpen as the project accumulates — Phase 1 questions are precise; later-phase questions are framed but final wording arrives the night-of, informed by what prior days revealed.

---

## Phase 1 — Origin & place (Days 1–15)
Birth, family architecture, childhood Bangkok, Pataiwodom Siksa primary school, the geography of memory before adolescence.

**Day 1.** Born 1981. That's the claim. We start there. Give me: day, month, year. Hospital. City. Who was in the room. The version your family tells of that day — verbatim if you remember the exact words, paraphrased if not. And one detail from that version that you've never quite believed.

**Day 2.** Your mother's full name in Thai script and romanization, her birth year, the city she was born in, what she did when you were five. Then: the one story she tells about you most often.

**Day 3.** Your father's full name, his profession at the time you were born, and the longest period he was ever absent from the household. What you remember about his return.

**Day 4.** Siblings. Names, birth order, current locations, occupations. The sibling you fought with most and what the fight was about — actually, not in the abstract.

**Day 5.** The first house you remember living in. Address if you can give it. The room you slept in. What was on the walls. Who else was in the house when you came home from school.

**Day 6.** Pataiwodom Siksa. Full Thai name of the school. The years you attended. Your daily walk to school — landmarks, distance, who you walked with. One teacher who saw you. One who didn't.

**Day 7.** Money in the household when you were eight. Were you rich, comfortable, getting by, struggling? What did your parents say versus what was actually true? The first time you understood what your family's money situation actually was.

**Day 8.** The first lie you remember telling. What you told, who you told it to, why you told it, whether you got caught. If you can't remember the first — the lie you've told most consistently across your life.

**Day 9.** Your earliest political memory. Not your political opinion — a moment when you understood that the country had a politics. Where you were, who told you, what they said.

**Day 10.** Religion in the household. Buddhist, animist, syncretic, ambivalent? What rituals were observed, what was performed for appearance, what was actually believed. Who in the family took it seriously.

**Day 11.** A childhood injury. The story you've told. The story your family tells. Where they diverge.

**Day 12.** Your first close friend. Their name. What happened to them. The last time you spoke.

**Day 13.** The first death that meant something to you. Whose, when, how you found out, what changed in the household after.

**Day 14.** A childhood obsession that adults dismissed but you still remember the texture of. Why you cared. What it told you about yourself.

**Day 15.** The day you understood you were going to leave Thailand someday. Where you were, what triggered it, whether you told anyone.

## Phase 2 — First mind (Days 16–25)
The books, films, music, intellectual hungers that predate university.

**Day 16.** The first book that hit you hard. Title, author, the age you read it, the line or scene that stayed.

**Day 17.** A film that changed how you saw something. Don't say "changed your life" — say what specific thing about the world you saw differently after.

**Day 18.** Music you loved in secret because your peers would have mocked it. What it was. Why you couldn't share it.

**Day 19.** The first intellectual you idolized. What you wanted from them. Whether you still respect them.

**Day 20.** A teacher in secondary school who shaped you. Their name, what they taught, what they actually taught beyond the subject.

**Day 21.** The first time you wrote something you were proud of. What it was. Whether it still exists. Whether it holds up.

**Day 22.** A debate you had with a parent that you remember winning. What you argued. Whether you'd still take that position.

**Day 23.** The first time you understood that the official story about Thailand wasn't the whole story. The trigger.

**Day 24.** Languages you tried to learn before you actually needed to. Which stuck, which didn't, why.

**Day 25.** *(theme-day, sharpened from prior answers)* — the intellectual posture you brought to university before university interrogated it.

---

## Phase 3 — Education abroad (Days 26–35)
MIT, Harvard, Oxford. Formation, disillusionment, contradictions.

**Day 26.** First application. Where you applied, where you got in, what you actually wanted versus what you said you wanted in the essay.

**Day 27.** Your first week abroad. The cold or heat. The food. The accent you couldn't parse. A specific scene.

**Day 28.** An advisor or professor at MIT who saw you. What they saw. Whether you've kept in touch.

**Day 29.** The intellectual position you went in with that didn't survive. What replaced it.

**Day 30.** Money abroad. Scholarship, family, debt, hustle? The cost of being where you were.

**Day 31.** Harvard versus MIT, in your direct experience. Not the rankings — the texture of the people.

**Day 32.** Oxford. What you went for and what you found. The English class system, encountered.

**Day 33.** A peer abroad who outclassed you intellectually. What they had that you didn't. Whether you've caught up.

**Day 34.** The credentials check moment (this is a fact-check day on Phase 3): the program names, degree titles, conferral years, advisors of record. I'll cross-reference.

**Day 35.** *(theme-day)* — what the degrees were actually for. And what they weren't.

## Phase 4 — Romantic life & intimacy (Days 36–50)
The loves, the parallel loves, the patterns, the costs. The phase requires consent thinking before it starts — see `docs/METHOD.md` for the redaction/pseudonym/withhold rules.

**Day 36.** The first time you fell in love. Age, place, their first name (or chosen pseudonym), what ended it.

**Day 37.** A relationship that ended because of you. What you did. Whether you'd do it again.

**Day 38.** The Bangkok 2006 girlfriend — the one whose house is near your current office. Sketch her — not for the public surface, for the archive. Then choose: pseudonym, first name only, or full redaction for the public version.

**Day 39.** Shanghai 2010. You wrote about the small apartment, the projector, the company. The truth, however you want to frame it.

**Day 40.** The first time you held two relationships at once. How it happened. What you told yourself it meant.

**Day 41.** The "love of my life" — you've used the phrase twice in 2018. Who, when, how each ended, and whether you'd still call them that.

**Day 42.** The pattern you can see in your relationships now that you couldn't see at twenty-five.

**Day 43.** Someone you wronged. What you did. What you've done about it. What you haven't.

**Day 44.** The relationship that taught you the most about yourself. What it taught.

**Day 45.** Family expectations about marriage. What you've delivered, what you've refused.

**Day 46.** Children — actual, planned, considered, refused. The honest version.

**Day 47.** Money in your relationships — who paid for what, where the resentments live, what you've learned.

**Day 48.** Jealousy. Yours, theirs, the asymmetry.

**Day 49.** A relationship currently active that you haven't named in any prior day. Or — the choice to keep one private. Either choice is fine; both get logged.

**Day 50.** *(theme-day)* — what intimacy is for, in your forty-fifth year.

## Phase 5 — Career arcs (Days 51–65)
NYU Shanghai, IDEO, Sydney University, Poland, Chengdu, Chiang Mai, current consultancies. Who you were at each.

**Day 51.** First real job. Title, salary, the one thing you learned that's still load-bearing.

**Day 52.** NYU Shanghai — title, dates, what you taught or researched, why you left.

**Day 53.** IDEO Shanghai — what you brought, what they wanted, what actually shipped.

**Day 54.** Sydney University — the year you quit. The specific moment that made the decision.

**Day 55.** Poland half-year — what looked like waste, what turned out to matter.

**Day 56.** Golden Panda Design Award judging — who else was on the panel, what won, whether the right thing won.

**Day 57.** Chiang Mai teaching — the institution, the students, the lessons that worked.

**Day 58.** A consulting client who hired you and then didn't listen to you. What they did instead. Whether they were right.

**Day 59.** Most you've ever been paid for a single piece of work. What it was. Whether you'd take that work again at any price.

**Day 60.** A failure professionally you've never fully reckoned with.

**Day 61.** A colleague you've lost touch with who you should call. Their name. Whether you will.

**Day 62.** A talent under you you should have promoted earlier.

**Day 63.** The architectural background — how it still shapes the way you think now. One specific example from the work you're doing this week.

**Day 64.** The anthropology background — same question, different lens.

**Day 65.** *(theme-day)* — the career you'd be having if you'd stayed in any one of the cities. Which one.

What each project in flight is actually *for*. Not the README answer — the real one.

**Day 66.** The Daoism reading site (dao.nonarkara.org). The audience you actually picture when you write for it. Are they real?

**Day 67.** The NSP concept prototype. What you want it to do that it isn't doing. Honest about why it isn't.

**Day 68.** The OpenClaw council. The bot you talk to most. What you talk about. Whether it's helping.

**Day 71.** TKC. The game-manual frame — does it actually change how people there work, or is it organizational poetry?

**Day 72.** The Chonburi / Bangkok / Thailand smart-city work. The client behind it. What you tell them versus what you actually believe about whether smart-city tech helps.

**Day 73.** The day trading dashboard. Are you trading. Honestly. What the P&L looks like.

**Day 74.** The 100 days of Non itself. Why you're doing this. Not the curated answer — the real one.

**Day 75.** *(theme-day)* — the project you've not started that you should have started by now.

## Phase 7 — Family & responsibility (Days 76–85)
Parents, siblings, what was given, what's owed, the choices ahead.

**Day 76.** Your parents now. Health. Where they live. How often you see them. What you've given them. What they need from you that you haven't given.

**Day 77.** A debt to a family member, financial or otherwise.

**Day 78.** A family secret you've kept. Whether you'll name it or keep it.

**Day 79.** The role you've ended up playing in the family. Whether you chose it.

**Day 80.** The next family obligation that's going to require something from you.

**Day 81.** Friends who've become family. Who.

**Day 82.** A friendship that's ended that you still grieve.

**Day 83.** Your obligations to Thailand — civic, professional, none. What you actually owe a place that made you.

**Day 84.** The student or mentee you've shaped most. Name. What they're doing now. Whether they know what they got from you.

**Day 85.** *(theme-day)* — the version of "responsibility" your parents would name versus the version you've built.

## Phase 8 — Death & meaning (Days 86–95)
The questions Isaacson rarely asks. Mortality, legacy, what's worth doing.

**Day 86.** The first time you understood you would die.

**Day 87.** The death that scares you most — yours, someone else's, a category.

**Day 88.** What you want done with your body.

**Day 89.** The thing you've not written that you'd regret not writing.

**Day 90.** A belief about meaning you held at twenty-five that you've abandoned.

**Day 91.** A belief about meaning you hold at forty-five that you suspect you'll abandon at sixty-five.

**Day 92.** The Daoist position on meaning, in your own words, not paraphrased from the text.

**Day 93.** The version of you that an obituary would describe. Versus the version you'd want.

**Day 94.** Someone you'd want at your funeral who probably won't come. Why.

**Day 95.** *(theme-day)* — what's worth doing in the next forty years, given the past forty.

## How the questions sharpen

Days 1–15 are precisely worded because we have prior context (the Pataiwodom Siksa mention, the World Cup memory thread, the family timeline he sketched). Phases 2 onward have framing but the night-of question incorporates what prior days revealed. The questions evolve with the project; the arc above is the spine.

## Phase 4 consent gate

Before Day 36, the project pauses. Non confirms the redaction policy: pseudonym, first name only, withhold-from-public, or full naming with verbatim consent from the named person. The choice is logged in the DEVLOG and visible on the public surface as part of the project's structural honesty. No surprises.
