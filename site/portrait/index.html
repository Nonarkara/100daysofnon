in his own words — never before in one place

Every line below is his, lifted word for word from the ninety-nine essays of the nonharvard blog. Each came from a different essay, about something else entirely — a bicycle ride, a cup of coffee, a dead philosopher, his father's grey hair. He wrote them years apart and never once set them side by side. The workshop did. This is the first time they stand in one room.

Colophon Twenty-one sentences · twelve essays · one voice. Lifted verbatim — spelling kept exactly as he wrote it. Chosen and set in order by the workshop; written, every word, years ago, by him. He has not read it in this order before. Neither, until now, has anyone.
