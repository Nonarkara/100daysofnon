## PHASE 0: Before Non — The family that made him possible
**~1910s–1981**

### CONFIRMED
- Maternal great-grandparents kept Suwan Panit (สุวรรณพานิช), a fabric and ready-made clothes shop in Sikhio, Nakhon Ratchasima. Chinese auspicious name above the door: 發祥. Registration paper: 1969 / พ.ศ. ๒๕๑๒. [diary day-001]
- Maternal grandmother: a Thai-Chinese woman from rural Nakhon Ratchasima. Never spent on herself. Bought a plot of suburban Bangkok land — years in advance — because she had decided all her children would one day leave Sikhio for the capital and would need ground to stand on. Paid for it; did not announce it. [diary day-001]
- Father: Kongkiet Arkaraprasertkul. Civil servant. The Arkaraprasertkul surname was invented — father (paternal grandfather of Non) looked up words he liked and constructed it, during the anti-Chinese-surname era, to secure registration and access to school. [blog day-47]
- Father born ~1948 (died at 62 in 2010; Non was ~29). [blog day-63/day-20]
- Mother: from Sikhio. Worked at Krung Thai Bank headquarters, Bangkok. [diary day-003]
- Non's full name: Non C. Arkaraprasertkul. The C is for Jamsuan — his mother's family name — carried quietly in the middle initial. In a single letter, both sides of the family. [diary day-001]

- Mother's parents = the grandparents who hosted Non in Sikhio every summer. [diary day-002]
- Paternal grandfather: unknown details. He created the surname; that is all that's confirmed.
- How parents met: unknown. Both were likely Bangkok-connected (father civil servant, mother working at KTB), but the meeting story is unrecorded.
