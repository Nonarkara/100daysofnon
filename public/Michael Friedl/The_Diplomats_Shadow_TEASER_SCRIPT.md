## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Visual Style
- **Primary palette:** Pure black, stark white, crimson red only
- **Lens:** Anamorphic widescreen (2.39:1 for theatrical, 9:16 vertical for social)
- **Lighting:** Extreme chiaroscuro — faces half in shadow, single light sources
- **Texture:** Film grain, slight flicker, cigarette smoke always present

### Audio Design
- **Score:** Industrial noir — think Trent Reznor meets Johann Johannsson
- **Piano:** Single notes, resonating, never a full melody
- **Bass:** Pulsing, irregular heartbeat rhythm
- **Silence:** Used as weapon — the moments between sounds are louder than the sounds

### Key Casting Notes
- **Dr. Non:** Thai actor, 30s, must convey intelligence and exhaustion simultaneously. Round glasses non-negotiable.
- **Michael Friedl (flashbacks):** Austrian actor, 50s, warm smile that haunts the film. Seen only in memories and surveillance photos.
- **Amel:** North African/Mediterranean actress, 30s, must switch between warm coach and cold strategist in the same scene.
- **Igor:** Eastern European actor, 40s, neutral expression — unreadable whether friendly or sinister.
- **Mariah:** Canadian actress, must maintain a smile that becomes unsettling the longer you look at it.

### Cost
- Estimated production: $2-3M (location-heavy: Bangkok, Vienna)
- Primary locations: Bangkok (Sathorn, RIEDEL, Austrian Embassy), Vienna (2-3 days)
- CGI minimal — practical effects, practical rain, practical shadows
