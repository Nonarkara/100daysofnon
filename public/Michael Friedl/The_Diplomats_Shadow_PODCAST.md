**DR. NON (V.O.):**
"He left too soon." Not "he passed away." Not "he died." Not "we lost him." "He left." The phrasing of someone who knew something was coming. Someone who had watched a departure. Someone who — and I'll get to this in a later episode — may have written those words before May 3rd.

**[MUSIC: Theme swells, then drops abruptly to silence.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**

About the 450 people who reacted to a memorial post and not one of them asked: "What happened?" About the 26 comments on a German-Thai Chamber post, all identical, all saying "heartfelt condolences," none saying "wait, how did he die?"

It's a story about how a man can die in Bangkok, and the internet can erase him, and his colleagues can move on, and his replacement can take his desk, and the world can keep spinning — as if Michael Friedl never existed at all.

I'm Dr. Non Arkaraprasertkul. And I refuse to let that happen.
