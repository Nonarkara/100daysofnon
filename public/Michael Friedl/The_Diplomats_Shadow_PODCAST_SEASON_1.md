# THE DIPLOMAT'S SHADOW
## A True Story About a Death Nobody Talks About
### Season One: The Erasure
### Hosted by Dr. Non Arkaraprasertkul

---

# EPISODE ONE: THE EMAIL

**[COLD OPEN]**

**[SOUND: Phone notification. An email arriving.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
May 5th, 2026. A Monday. I'm at my desk at DEPA — the Digital Economy Promotion Agency — working on a dashboard project. My phone buzzes. An email.

The subject line says: "We Mourn the Loss of Michael Friedl, Austrian Commercial Counsellor."

The sender is ADVANTAGE AUSTRIA Bangkok.

I read the email three times before I understand what it means.

**[SOUND: Email being opened. Screen reading. Then — silence. Heavy silence.]**

**IGOR LUKIC (Recorded, reading the actual email):**
*"Dear Dr. Arkara,

We are deeply saddened to share the heartbreaking news that our Austrian Commercial Counsellor, Michael Friedl, has passed away suddenly and unexpectedly on Sunday, 3 May 2026 in Thailand.

We mourn an outstanding Head of ADVANTAGE AUSTRIA Thailand, a highly respected colleague, and a dear friend. His leadership, dedication, and personality have left a lasting impact on all of us.

Our thoughts and heartfelt condolences are with his wife Amel and the entire family during this incredibly difficult time.

During this challenging period, please rest assured that the team of ADVANTAGE AUSTRIA Thailand and I remain at your disposal.

Best regards,

Igor Lukic
Austrian Commercial Attaché"*

**[SOUND: The email closing. Silence. Then — a deep breath.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
That's it. That's the entire notification. Four paragraphs from a man I'd never met — Igor Lukic — telling me my friend was dead. "Passed away suddenly and unexpectedly." That's the phrase. That's all I got. No cause of death. No circumstances. No "here's what happened." Just: "suddenly and unexpectedly."

**[SOUND: Phone being set down. A chair creaking.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
Michael and I had been texting for a year. Since late 2024, when he arrived in Bangkok. We'd never quite managed to meet in person — I kept missing events, he kept being patient about it — but we were friends. Real friends. We joked about my terrible punctuality. He sent me photos from Koh Chang. I sent him screenshots of my dashboards. We talked about Austria and Thailand and where to find decent schnitzel in Bangkok.

And now I was reading about his death in a form letter from his replacement.

**[SOUND: WhatsApp scrolling. Message sounds. Faint laughter.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
Let me take you back. Because I need you to understand who Michael was before I tell you what happened to him.

**[MUSIC: Theme — sparse piano, single notes, melancholic. Five seconds.]**

**[TITLE CARD]**

**ANNOUNCER:**
The Diplomat's Shadow. A true story. Episode One: The Email.

**[MUSIC FADES]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
I'm Dr. Non Arkaraprasertkul. I'm a Thai civil servant. Senior Expert at DEPA. I have degrees from Harvard, MIT, and Oxford, but what I actually do is build things that vendors say can't be built. When three software houses told the Thai government they couldn't build a Middle East monitoring dashboard, I built it myself. From 10 queries a day to 2,000. The one-person service. That's me.

But this story isn't about dashboards. It's about Michael Friedl.

**[SOUND: WhatsApp message opening. Phone scrolling.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
Michael and I met — properly — in late 2024. He'd just been posted to Bangkok as the new Head of ADVANTAGE AUSTRIA Thailand. Austrian Commercial Counsellor. We were introduced at a depa event. Quick handshake. Name tags. Business cards. The usual.

But the real connection happened over text. Over the next year, we kept in touch. He invited me to events. I kept missing them. It became a running joke.

**[SOUND: WhatsApp messages being read, Michael's voice warm and American-accented]**

**MICHAEL (WhatsApp audio, warm, friendly):**
*"You're welcome! Good that we had time for a good catch up. You're back Friday. Let's schedule a rendez-vous before you leave."*

**DR. NON (WhatsApp audio, slightly embarrassed):**
*"Let's do it!"*

**MICHAEL (laughing):**
*"You are really having a dramatic life!"*

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
That's who he was. Warm. Funny in this understated way. Michael had lived in America for years — his name was technically German pronunciation, but he went by "Michael" because he'd been in the States so long. He texted like a friend, not a diplomat. He remembered things. He followed up. He was the kind of person who sent you photos of empty chairs with captions like "Saved you a seat."

November 2025. We finally had dinner. RIEDEL restaurant. The selfie — me, Michael, a colleague. Wine glasses. Warm smiles. That was the last time I saw him alive.

**[SOUND: Camera shutter. A photograph. Silence.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
After that, the messages slowed. Not because anything was wrong. Life gets busy. December. January. The occasional check-in. Then in April 2026, something changed.

April 12th. Songkran. I'm at Koh Chang. I text him: "Not too hot?"

He replies: "Not too hot, actually very pleasant." Then: "How is koh chang?"

And Michael says: "Very very pleasant. Great to calm down. We should go there together. You should be in the loop and help me see the potential of tourism and digital nomads!"

That was it. The last real conversation. After that — silence.

**[SOUND: Ocean waves. Distant. Fading.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
April 26th. I text him: "Let me know if you go to HK. But I'll be here for sure when you're back!"

No reply.

I didn't think much of it. People don't reply sometimes. They're busy. Traveling. Living their lives. It never occurred to me that Michael Friedl was already dying.

**[SOUND: Silence. Then — the notification sound again. The email.]**

**IGOR LUKIC (Recorded):**
*"...has passed away suddenly and unexpectedly on Sunday, 3 May 2026 in Thailand."*

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
May 5th. Two days after he died. I learn about it from a form letter. Signed by Igor Lukic, the man who replaced him.

I stared at that email for a long time. "Suddenly and unexpectedly." I read those words over and over. In my experience — and I've traveled, I've been to many places — when someone dies, especially a premature death, there's always a reason. People tell you. "Heart attack." "Car accident." "Cancer." Something. There's closure.

This email gave me nothing. Just: "suddenly and unexpectedly."

**[SOUND: Typing on a laptop. A search being entered.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
I started searching. Michael's LinkedIn — gone. 404 error. His Facebook — deleted. His ADVANTAGE AUSTRIA profile — removed. The Wayback Machine — zero snapshots. In 72 hours, every trace of Michael Friedl had been scrubbed from the internet.

I found one article. Krone.at, May 5th. Six paragraphs. "Osterreicher Diplomat in Bangkok gestorben." It quoted a "close confidant" who said Michael was "very likeable and extraordinarily competent." It did not mention a cause of death. It did not mention where he was found. It did not mention whether there was an autopsy. Six paragraphs. That's all.

**[SOUND: Paper rustling. Silence.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
And then I found the memorial posts. LinkedIn. 26 comments on the German-Thai Chamber post. 510 reactions on a journalist's post. And I read every single one.

"My heartfelt condolences."  
"So sorry for your loss."  
"Rest in peace."  
"Our thoughts are with you."  

Every single one identical. Every single one empty. Not one person asked: "What happened?" Not one person said: "How did he die?" Not one person questioned "suddenly and unexpectedly."

**[SOUND: Rain on glass. Bangkok traffic. Distant.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
That's when I knew I had to ask. Because if nobody else was going to, I would.

I'd been to many places in the world. And everywhere, when someone dies young, there's a reason. That's closure. That's what humans need. And this — this email, these empty condolences, this silence — it wasn't closure. It was the opposite. It was a wall. A wall built out of politeness and protocol and "suddenly and unexpectedly."

And I decided to climb it.

**[MUSIC: Theme returns, building. Then — cuts to silence.]**

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
Next time. I reach out to Michael's friends. And one of them tells me something that changes everything.

**[SOUND: Signal message notification.]**

**ELIOT (Recorded, shocked):**
*"He was in hospital a few weeks ago for something small.. I spoke with Amel weeks ago? No way wtf. I wouldn't jump into a conclusion that it was the... Probably best to ask his wife about it."*

**DR. NON (V.O.):**
Episode Two: The Hospital.

**[MUSIC: Theme swells. Cuts. End of episode.]"
