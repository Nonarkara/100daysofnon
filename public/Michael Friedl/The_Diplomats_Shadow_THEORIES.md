## THEORY 1: POST-SURGICAL PULMONARY EMBOLISM — THE MEDICAL NEGLIGENCE COVER-UP

**Probability: HIGH**

**What happened:**

Michael broke his ankle (possibly during Songkran celebrations at Koh Chang, April 12). He underwent surgery at a Bangkok hospital in late April. The surgery required anesthesia and sedation. Post-surgery, he was immobilized. A blood clot formed in his leg (Deep Vein Thrombosis — DVT). The clot traveled to his lungs on May 3. Pulmonary Embolism (PE). Sudden death within minutes.

**Why the cover-up:**

If the hospital failed to administer standard DVT prophylaxis (blood thinners), this is **medical malpractice**. An Austrian diplomat dying from preventable negligence at a Bangkok hospital becomes an **international incident**. Both governments would want to suppress it.

- The hospital doesn't want liability
- The Austrian government doesn't want to admit they failed to ensure adequate care
- The Thai government doesn't want a diplomatic scandal
- WKO doesn't want questions about duty of care
- Amel may have been offered a settlement in exchange for silence
- "Operation Clean Sweep" was the erasure protocol to bury the malpractice

**Why it fits the evidence:**
- The ankle surgery is confirmed (Eliot, via Amel)
- The timeline fits perfectly: surgery late April → death May 3
- "Suddenly and unexpectedly" is exactly how PE presents
- The erasure protects the hospital
- The silence serves all institutional interests
- Amel's silence = settlement/NDA or legal advice

**Why it might not be the full story:**
- It doesn't explain Michael's UN space law background
- It doesn't explain why Dr. Non is under surveillance
- It doesn't explain why the erasure was so total and so fast
- A malpractice cover-up is serious but doesn't usually require deleting someone's entire existence
