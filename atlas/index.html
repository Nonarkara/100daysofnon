The Atlas

the life as a single line

One route, thirteen stops. It leaves Bangkok in 1981 and, forty-five years and three continents later, arrives back in Bangkok. The cities he returned to — Bangkok, Boston, Shanghai — are drawn as interchanges: a hollow ring means he had stood there before. The line is not drawn to scale. No life is.

Colophon Thirteen stops, drawn from the timeline the workshop keeps. Three cities are visited more than once — Bangkok, Boston, Shanghai — and marked as returns. The dates are from the record; where the record is thin, the year is the one he has given and not yet revised. A man who spent his youth trying to leave one city ends the line back inside it.
