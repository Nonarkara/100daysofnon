# Artifact — Japan 2006 Photo Bundle (4 individual photos)

**Source:** Non's phone photo album, accessed 2026-05-27 (sent in two batches: the thumbnail-grid screenshot first, then 5 individual photos — one of which duplicated the night-street one already filed)
**Trip date:** 2006-11-03 and surrounding days

These 4 photos individually capture moments from the Japan 2006 trip described in Day 3 Messages 2, 5, and 6.

---

## `2006-11-japan-with-father-temple.png`

**Subjects:** Non (~21, sunglasses, longer hair, black jacket, camera around his neck) + **father** in a pinstripe business suit and tie.
**Setting:** Buddhist temple courtyard, daytime. Pagoda visible in background, recognizable Asakusa Sensoji-style architecture.
**Significance:** The photographic core of the trip. The hug-first-thing-when-I-saw-him reunion described in Msg 2 happened earlier in the week at the Chiba hotel; this photo is the daytime father-son sightseeing the day(s) after. Father's title at the time: Deputy Director General of the Department of Local Administration, with the off-season Buriram governorship appointment imminent.

## `2006-11-japan-with-father-bench.png`

**Subjects:** Non + father, both in matching black short-sleeve shirts, seated together on a black bench in a stone-floored lobby (hotel or airport).
**Setting:** transit / waiting moment, daylight.
**Significance:** quieter, more casual register than the temple photo. Both wearing similar outfits — an unselfconscious father-son visual register the Msg 5 confirmation of the November dates makes more poignant (Non's dad would die in September 2010, less than 4 years later).

## `2006-11-japan-with-kengo-kuma.png`

**Subjects:** Non (right, dark suit, glasses, hand resting on what appears to be an architecture monograph or portfolio) + **Kengo Kuma** (left, slightly older Japanese man with a thoughtful posture, hand near face).
**Setting:** modern Tokyo interior at night — low table, contemporary chairs in the background, designer space (possibly Kuma's own studio or a hotel he designed).
**Significance:** the **midnight meeting with Kengo Kuma** Non flagged in Msg 6. Kuma is — as Non noted — *"now a very famous architect, he designed almost every building in Japan right now"* (his portfolio post-2006 includes the V&A Dundee, Japan National Stadium for the 2020 Olympics, the Nezu Museum, hundreds of major projects). In 2006 he was already established but pre-stadium-fame. **The connection runs through the Asian Cultural Council** — both Non and Kuma are ACC alumni, which Non described in Msg 2 as the "complete loop" closing.

## `2006-11-japan-non-crashed-hotel-bed.png`

**Subject:** Non passed out on a small Japanese business hotel bed, fully clothed in black, on his side, dress shoes and backpack still on the bed/floor.
**Setting:** small hotel room with off-white walls and minimal furniture.
**Significance:** almost certainly the **Chiba-hotel crash** described in Msg 2 — after the long izakaya night with the unnamed senior from college who *"bought me out and dropped me off at the hotel where my dad was supposed to stay."* The senior dropped him off; he made it to the room; he passed out clothed. The "one-hour train ride to the actual hotel in Chiba" came the next morning. This photo is the *evidence* of the drinking night that preceded the reunion.

## Why these photos matter together

This is the first time the project has photographic-level evidence of Non with his father, the father's physical appearance, the specific places they were together (temple, hotel lobby, Tokyo at night), and the *intellectual figure* (Kuma) Non encountered on the same trip via the ACC funding loop. Combined with the Msg 5 date correction, the November 3 timestamp, and the off-season Buriram appointment hypothesis, the trip is now reconstructed to a precision the project should keep — this is the most well-documented week of pre-2010 father-son time on the record.
