## What this artifact establishes

This is the article Non flagged in Day 3 Msg 6 — *"There was an article that I wrote for the booklet that we give to people who came to our dad's funeral. I wrote about how I got to Harvard from a small, little technical college in the suburb of Bangkok."* Found, verified, archived by reference (full text remains at the WordPress URL; reproducing here would duplicate without adding signal).

The 2018 republication adds an annotated preface by 28→37-year-old Non reflecting on how his understanding has shifted in the 8 years since writing it.

## Facts corroborated from the article (already on the project record)

- **Father's full name:** นายก้องเกียรติ อัครประเสริฐกุล (Mr. Kongkiet Arkaraprasertkul) — matches the Day 1/2 record
- **AFS in Oklahoma 1998–1999 (Thai BE 2541–2542)** — confirms the "American Field Service" reading; the "American Fulbright service program" in Day 3 Msg 3 was a voice-transcript conflation of AFS + Fulbright (two distinct programs Non held a decade apart)
- **KMITL Faculty of Architecture, 6 years** (1998–2004 calendar years, including the AFS gap year)
- **Thesis "barely passed"** (ผ่านอย่างคาบเส้น) — confirms the Day 3 Msg 3 B-grade humiliation
- **MIT master's started 2005** in **History, Theory, Criticism of Architecture (HTC)** — taken on Fulbright (covered half tuition) + MIT scholarship (covered the other half)
- **Master's thesis on Shanghai's urban history** — later published (book listed on Amazon, title *Shanghai's Contemporary*)
- **Oxford for PhD** in history / politics / modern China studies (after a stint as MIT visiting lecturer)
- **PhD completed by ~2016** (article notes "PhD completed 2 years ago" from the 2018 dateline)

## New facts surfaced (not previously on the project record)

- **Thammasat University lecturer** between KMITL and MIT — *"ตอนนั้นผมเป็นอาจารย์ประจำที่มหาวิทยาลัยธรรมศาสตร์"* — Non held a Thammasat lectureship when he applied to MIT. This refines the academic-career sequence: KMITL → Thammasat → MIT → Oxford → Harvard → (Shanghai NYU 3 yrs) → return → Chula. The Day 2 mention of being a "professor right away after graduation" likely refers to Thammasat, not Chula.
- **20 scholarships applied to; 1 received** — Fulbright was the only one that came through, after 19 rejections. This is the source of the "leave nothing on the table" applications philosophy.
- **MIT first-semester struggle** — Non was below the threshold to continue after his first semester at MIT; the institute gave him one extra semester to prove himself as a foreign student. This is a *new* hardship-detail Day 3 didn't surface.
- **UC Berkeley was the backup** — if MIT funding hadn't materialized, Non would have gone to Berkeley (cheaper public-system tuition, Fulbright would have covered it).
- **Last-semester MIT shopping-period story** — Non emailed a Japanese-culture professor, ended up taking a course on modern Japanese history through photography and print media — the *intellectual* origin of the November 2006 Japan trip's seriousness.
- **Worked across four continents:** Asia, Europe, America, Australia (the article's 2018 self-summary).
- **Two podcast interviews exist** about the same arc — biographical source material that could be cross-referenced for fact-check.
