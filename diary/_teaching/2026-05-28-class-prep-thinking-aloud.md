## Extracted structure — the lecture outline as it stands

**Opening — don't patronize.** Audience has 2+ years of ChatGPT exposure. Start where they are.

**Section 1 — AI is already around you.** Examples to anchor:
- Netflix recommendation algorithm (the "early AI" reference)
- Shopee product recommendations
- TikTok feed
- Mall license-plate cameras

**Section 2 — The three types of training.**

| Type | Non's framing | Real-world example to show |
|---|---|---|
| **Supervised** | "Teach the AI that this is 5, this is 6, this is cat, this is dog" — rule-based, classification | License plate cameras at malls; spam filters |
| **Unsupervised** | AI spots patterns in pixels / formations humans don't have time to find | Recommendation engines; anomaly detection |
| **Reinforcement** | AI makes a mistake, you correct it, AI reshapes its performance | ChatGPT and other generative AI |

**Section 3 — Demystification.** *"AI is not magic. There's a system behind it."* This is the rhetorical pivot.

**Section 4 — Non's operating mental model.** Borrowed/refined from the Super Intelligence podcast he listened to this morning:
- *"I have ten assistants, and these are good assistants. How would I ask them to do the work for me?"*
- AI = people / departments / bureaus / experts in different fields
- Orchestration determines quality
- Hallucination = AI taking shortcuts; human sensibility is the corrective
- Agentic AI = tool calls on your behalf (going beyond response)

**Section 5 — Dashboards demo.** Show his own work. *"Before I show the dashboard, or maybe I just show the dashboard along the way."*
