## Non's framing (verbatim)

> This was another fun episode of my life back in 2018, pretty much after I left the University of Sydney. I still didn't know why I left the university. The plan was that I would just do the minimum, try to blend in, and I did that so well when I joined Thammasat University in 2004 as a junior faculty member. I just blended in, taught minimally, did the minimum, and flew by, and then went to MIT afterwards and never looked back.

> I could have done the same, and within maybe a couple of years I could have been a resident and maybe I would get a passport. An Australian passport is definitely stronger than a Thai passport, but of course I didn't want to play the game. I didn't want to understand the game. I didn't want to just get stuck intellectually, so I decided not to stay.

> I had a semester, thanks to my friend Garcia, who contacted me and said she had a visiting professorship from the fund that the director, the president of the university himself, allocated to the Oriental Institute that she was a lecturer in, if I wanted to come and teach. I didn't know how big Poland was going to be. I thought, Poland? Really? I went anywhere because I had nowhere to go, and then I was blown away by the beauty of Kraków, where I taught. I was at Jagiellonian University, one of the top universities, and I even dated a girl named Martina, but at the end I decided not to go any further because she tends to be... Idk, maybe I was rather alone than be with a girl that doesn't understand who I was.

> Anyway, picture from one of the house party that I hosted, where all the friends came over from all over the world for:
> - Christopher from the U.S.
> - Akeen from the Netherlands
> - Max from Latvia
> - Garcia, of course, locally
> and then we had a couple of friends from China. It was a fun night here at my apartment.
