# Day 1 — Narration

Day one. He gave a date, a hospital, an area, a brother six years older, a house his father built. The calendar agreed: November 1st, 1981 was a Sunday. The hospital exists at the address the geography implied. The area is plausible. One detail tripped — he placed his infancy "near Siam Paragon," a mall that wouldn't exist for another twenty-four years. Not a memory error. A grammar error. The system logged it anyway because that is the system.

He pushed back. Cleared his throat in writing — *Ahem!* — and went back in time. Where Siam Paragon now stands, there was a hotel. The Siam Intercontinental. A roof shaped like a hat. An American architect in the Wright tradition. Gardens. His mother's dry cleaner. Wedding receptions. An Australian student-affairs expo in 2002 — the last event he attended there before they tore it down. He gave it all back to the page with the precision of someone who had been waiting for a system patient enough to ask.

Then the geography expanded outward from the row house. The elephant-headed bridge over the small canal. The crossing to Sra Pathum Palace. The beer garden. The science center. The walk to Siam Square — three theatres still standing then, Scala and Lido and Siam. His mother's commute to Krung Thai Bank on Sukhumvit. The maternal grandmother who saw the city coming, who bought the land before the kids needed it, who was stingy in the way that produces house. The eminent domain that took the land away and produced the money that produced the house he's been in for forty-two years.

And the mall. He named it. Pantip Plaza. A small body in a baby carrier, lost in classical columns, found by mother and brother. Doric, Ionic, learned years later. The acoustics still there in him.

A second slip surfaced in the expansion: Frank Lloyd Wright did not have a Paris style. The system logged that one too. Two slips in two messages — not because Non remembers badly. Because at the level of specificity Non thinks at, the language reaches for nearby anchors that aren't quite the right anchors. The fact-check shows where the reach happens. That is the mechanic.

What the day established: the project's premise holds. The mirror is honest in both directions. The mirror also pushes back, and Non answers the push by giving more.
